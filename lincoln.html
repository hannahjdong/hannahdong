Lincoln Private Jets

  • Role:

  • UI/UX Designer

  • Timeline:

  • Jun. 2021 - Sep. 2021

  • Skills:

  • Research, UI/UX Design, Prototyping

  • Tools Used:

  • Figma, Adobe XD

Last summer, I designed the mobile application and website for the start-up Lincoln Private Jets. Cleverly nicknamed “the next Uber of private jets,” Lincoln Private Jets wishes to revolutionize private jet booking. As the UI/UX designer, I collaborated across disciplines with both engineers and business partners.

The Problem

The private aviation world is relatively unknown to the general public and with growing concerns over public health, people are looking for safer ways to fly.

The Design Process

User Analysis

Personas
  • John
  • Goal:

  • Use a jet booking service that meets his needs

  • Pain Points:

  • Accessibility: He wants to have a jet booking app that he can use anywhere

  • Membership Options: John lives a luxury lifestyle and would like services to accommodate his extravagant purchases

  • Brian
  • Goal:

  • Find high-end services to promote to his large following

  • Pain Points:

  • Sustainability: Brian cares about the environment and is conscious about his carbon footprint

  • Efficiency: He lives a busy lifestyle and would like an easy to use service

Task Analysis

With the personas in mind, I created 3 high-level tasks:

  1. Book a Private Charter

  2. Explore FlightPool (Shared Flights)

  3. Find Member Benefits

Design Workflows

Book a Private Charter

The main feature of the mobile application is booking a private flight. Through the personas, we understand two major pain points are the complexity of booking flights and the busyness of individual users. With a straightforward booking system, the app allows users to book with ease and complete the steps on the go.

Explore FlightPool (Shared Flights)

One prominent feature I designed for the application is called “FlightPool.” FlightPool allows users to propose and share flights with others. The user can find proposed flights and filter the flights by departure, arrival, and more. If there are no flights available, the user will be prompted to propose a flight or receive an alert.

Member Benefits Walkthrough

The Solution

Thus, our application creates a more equitable and accessible space for users to fly anytime, anywhere. The sleek design prioritizes efficiency and our main app features emphasize sustainability and comfort.

Concluding Thoughts

I feel incredibly grateful to have had the opportunity to work for a small start-up with an amazing team. The start-up environment is one filled with creativity, ambition, and lots of fun. I am excited to continue to grow and expand my skillset.
